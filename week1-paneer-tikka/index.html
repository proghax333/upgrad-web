Paneer Tikka Masala

Prep: 20 mins   |   Cook: 30 mins   |   Serves: 3–4

Reference: Paneer Tikka Masala Recipe - Swasthi's Recipes

Ingredients

For the paneer tikka (marinade)

  • 300 g paneer, cut into 2 cm cubes
  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1–2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1 small bell pepper & 1 small onion, cut into chunks for skewering

For the masala (gravy)

  1. 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  2. 1 bay leaf (optional)
  3. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  4. 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  5. 2 large tomatoes, pureed (or 1 cup tomato puree)
  6. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  7. 1 tsp red chili powder
  8. 1 tsp coriander powder
  9. 1/2–1 tsp garam masala
  10. 1/4 cup cashew paste or 2 tbsp heavy cream (for richness)
  11. 1/2 cup water (adjust for desired consistency)
  12. Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  13. Salt to taste

Method

1. Marinate & cook paneer

  1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, lemon juice, oil and salt to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Add paneer cubes (and bell pepper/onion chunks if using). Gently toss to coat. Marinate for 15–30 minutes (or up to 2 hours in fridge).
  3. Thread paneer (and veggies) onto skewers or place on a baking tray. Cook under a hot grill/oven at 200°C (400°F) for 8–10 minutes, turning once, until edges char slightly. Alternatively, pan-fry in a little oil until golden. Remove and set aside.

2. Make the masala

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add bay leaf (if using) and sauté briefly. Add chopped onion and cook until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until raw smell disappears.
  3. Stir in tomato puree and cook until oil begins to separate from the masala (6–8 minutes).
  4. Add turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder and salt. Cook 1–2 minutes to roast the spices.
  5. Blend the cooked mixture for a smooth gravy if you prefer a silky texture (optional).
  6. Return the gravy to pan, add cashew paste or cream, and 1/2 cup water. Simmer for 4–5 minutes to reach the desired consistency.
  7. Add cooked paneer tikka pieces to the gravy and simmer gently for 2–3 minutes so the flavours meld. Sprinkle garam masala and stir gently.

To Serve

Garnish with chopped coriander and a drizzle of cream (optional). Serve hot with naan, roti, paratha, or steamed basmati rice.

Tips & Variations

  • Use store-bought paneer or make fresh paneer; pat dry before marinating so it soaks less marinade and holds shape.
  • For smoky flavour, char the cooked paneer briefly over an open flame or add a drop of liquid smoke to the gravy.
  • Make it vegan by substituting paneer with firm tofu and using cashew cream or coconut cream instead of dairy.
  • If gravy is too tangy, add a pinch of sugar to balance flavours.

Simple, restaurant-style Paneer Tikka Masala — quick to make and great for guests.
